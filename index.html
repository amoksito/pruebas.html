Geometrical proofs are conclusive, and not a matter of opinion. It can be proved that in Euclidean geometry the sum of the interior angles of a plane triangle equals two right angles and that a denial of this proposition entails a contradiction. Assuming the character of a demonstrative deductive science, geometry proves to correspond to, and to realize the idea of epistémé; it appears as the model and the standard of knowledge worthy of that name.
